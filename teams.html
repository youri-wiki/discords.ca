Rooftops & Alleys Teams lore

To better understand the history of teams, we first need to look at the current era of the game. The earliest teams emerged in a solo game that was originally meant to remain that way forever. Players were left alone with vast maps and no one but themselves, no tags, no trick battles, just lines. As a result, people became creative and developed techniques far more advanced than we could have ever imagined.

Momentum.

Team Momentum, co-created by Nerve and Libo, was the first team; it was early. Momentum had a mission to regroup the best players. At this time, the best ones were those who found tech and made lines with those. They would gatekeep the techs among themselves and show them off all at once, claiming the techs at the same time. Momentum had no competition; it was THE team. They produced a few videos and discovered tons of tech that is still there today. Momentum members were hungry for tech and content, but their leader wasn’t, he was waiting. Players took the lead, left, and killed Momentum to create something bigger.

Illusion. [ILSN]

Team Illusion, created by Gen, was born shortly after Momentum. People started liking the concept of teams, probably to cope with the lack of multiplayer. While Momentum was the big tech team, Illusion was only focused on line hitting with tech, no tech hunting or gatekeeping. Tech lines at its root, putting a little pressure on Momentum, reminding them “We are there too now.” If you were good but not Momentum good, Illusion was the team for you. The team sadly got disbanded by its leader because people among the team started drama with each other and RNA staff.

Realism. [RLSM], z

Realism was mostly a joke, since at this time all the teams were tech oriented. The team idea was: You are not that good of a player? You don’t know any tech? Just put the team tag in your name and you are in. Simple as that. Just like any joke, the team started to fade away with the joke.

Motion.

Motion was created by Momentum's members co-founded by Nerve and Sev. Being tired of their inactive leader, they made the decision to leave and make their own thing. Motion kept up their reputation, being the most active team content-wise and member-wise. They had the best line hitters, with Nerve standing out as one of the greatest of all time, and when the multiplayer and tag update was released, they had some pretty decent tag players. Even though they weren’t an official team from the start, they officially became one shortly after. Motion was for a long time the main tournament host and hosted the biggest tournament since. Motion eventually got disbanded due to some drama and inactivity.

Multiplayer update

Following up with the time line, multiplayer update released somewhere in the middle of motion history. From now on, teams objective wasn't to have the best line hitter, but to have the best tag players.

Edgerunner.

Edgerunners originated from a group of friends. When they learned that the game would receive a multiplayer update, they created a group chat. Later, Edgerunners became an official team and earned the title of the first “official team” of RnA leaded by Roblox noob . The team has always maintained its “friend group” identity, never really producing content and remaining invite-only. They teased a team intro for a long time, but it was never released, which became a meme within the community. Still waiting for the video bikler. They still exist today, but they are no longer active.

Verve.

Verve emerged when a player named Bloxul saw Edgerunner and Motion. He saw an opportunity, the opportunity to regroup players and become better together. Verve eventually got a decent tag player base, which includes Dante, one of the best players of all time. They weren’t humble about it, which led to some toxicity and gave them a bad reputation. Throughout the time they managed to redeem themselves and get rid of that reputation. For a long time, they were the main tourney host before the team slowly became inactive even though some players are still there.

Somewhen between Escape key and verve creation. Two teams named Chemical and Unbeatable were created. I don't have enough info to tell you more. But they were there.

Escape Key. [ESC]

Escape keys, created by Topher, is the first keyboard and mouse only team. They went on a journey to popularize the usage of keyboards and mouse by staying on the competitive side of the game. The team proved that keyboard and mouse was an option too when a member won a tourney against the most popular and hyped-up controller player using a tech only doable on keyboard and mouse. Escape key eventually became the main tourney host even though most of their players went inactive
